Designing a website for an airline comes with specific requirements and considerations, as it could be used by thousands of people from different backgrounds. Find out how I created the layout and visual design for FlyUX, a concept project for a new face in the airline industry which aims to beat the competition by means of trustworthy and usable design, accessible useflow, and a no nonsense booking process.

Homepage &
Booking Form
Exploration

In these drafts build in Balsamiq, I explore the form and layout of the homepage and the booking form as a primary component of the page.

Booking Form

The booking form adapts to the various stages, witholding information until necessary and guiding the user through an easy start of the booking process with the help of the primary blue tint.

Homepage

The FlyUX homepage emphasis the booking form, customer support, and travel inspiration. Each of these functions is within immediate reach when the customer needs them. A centerpiece searchbar allows customers to find information on various topics and questions.

Ticket Search
Exploration

Interaction was a quality I wanted to implement in the search result list of tickets from early on. The challenge was to find an implementation that did not impede the quality of the user experience. Tickets have an initial stage that displays the most important information, as well as an expanded hover stage with more details about the ticket.

To save the user time on additional stages, selection of fare type and luggage is handled within the interactive ticket panel.

Ticket Search Final

Ticket selection is separated by out- and inbound flight and the whole process of selection of flight, fare type and luggage is completed on one page. Centering relevant information and the use of the primary blue tint helps keep the user on track.

Personal Details

On the personal details section the user is asked to fill in the passenger detail forms. Here the user also has the choice to further engage with the company, sign up for updates, or opt-out completely.

Seat selection, Overview & Payment

Once they've selected their ideal flights, the customer is just a few steps away from obtaining the tickets. The user can select their seating per flight, or skip to an overview of their selections. Here the user can change details of their tickets or add any extras, before finalizing the process at the payment page.

Purchase Collection

As a confirmation of their purchase, after completion of the transaction the user is send to the ticket collection section. Here they are congratulated on their future travel plans, shown details of their tickets and presented with localized information of their destination

Besides, the user also has the option to further engage by creating an account, making any possible amends to their order, can have their itinerary send by email, or, if available, the user can proceed to check in online.
